Deep Learning Model Analysis and Visualization

During my internship at LaBri for the Universit√© de Bordeaux, I worked on a deep learning model analysis and visualization project. The main goal was to gain insights into the hidden layers of an AI Deep Learning model and to make it easy to visually see what happens in those layers. It will also help to improve understanding and performance. In the visualization, you can see all the layers of a model alongside their activations (outputs of a layer) grouped into clusters. It is interesting to see in what layer a model makes the correct prediction and when it goes wrong because this can help developers identify which layers need improvement.

Project 4.0

This was a big group project for a real company. We had to find a solution for the problem that they were experiencing. They had too little electric chargers for the amount of electric vehicles the employees have. We were a team of 5 students. I took care of the AI part of the application and the documentation. For the AI part I made sure there was data coming in from a camera that checked if there was a car parked on a specific parking spot or not. This is an indication whether there are chargers free or not. This data was used to show on a map which spot was free and which wasn't free. There was also a visualization made with this data. The time of the rush hours. So, you could see that for example there were not that many free spots on a Monday morning at 9, so that is a rush hour. I used a yolo model that I trained further myself as well and computer vision to check the pixels. If there are enough pixels and it detects a car in the parking spot, it will say that it is occupied.

I learned to work together with students that are in a different field within the Applied Computer Science program. There were students from application development but also from cyber security. It was interesting because we all know other things and can make a whole project together. I also learned more about the communication with a real client.

AI

For our AI course, we had 3 smaller individual projects. The first one was around general AI, the second one about Machine Learning and the last one about Deep Learning. All three of these projects were showed in a streamlit app. You can find screenshots of those websites when you click the button. The first project was a program for solving a cryptarithmetic puzzle. An example of this is SEND + MORE = MONEY. For this I used the simpleai library.

The second project was about benchmarking two Machine Learning algorithms. I chose to use a dataset about estimation of obesity levels based on eating habits, physical condition, smoker... The goal is to classify the level of obesity. I used Random Forest, AdaBoost and KNeighbors to compare.

For the last project that was about Deep Learning, more specific image classification, I chose to scrape images of marine life. The classes I chose were dolphins, sea turtles, star fish, clown fish and jelly fish. I trained a CNN on that dataset but the result was not the best.

From these small projects, I learned the basics of AI and what is possible. I learned how to create and train models but also how to use streamlit.

Requirement Analysis

During the project week for the Requirement Analysis course, our team of six people did the analysis of the requirements our client provided. We started by reviewing the reports of the other students who had the same assignment during the first semester. Then we had a short meeting with the client to ask questions. After that it was time to make the use case diagram (first picture) and a data model (second picture) with the team. Then we started the individual work, namely we started working on the prototypes. This is what the application we are going to create should look like. I have attached the pictures of some of the prototypes I made. We had a team meeting to discuss the look and functions of the prototypes to make them look similar, and we made a few agreements and changes. Once that was finished, we could start working on the document, where we listed all the functionalities from the use case diagram with the data model view and the prototype.

I learned how to talk with a client in a professional way, but also how to translate the customer's requirements into actual functionalities and prototypes.
